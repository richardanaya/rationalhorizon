Week 1: What Scientists Do

Using Our Senses to Explore the World

‚Üê Back to Grade 1 Science

Week Learning Goals

  • Use all five senses to make careful observations
  • Ask scientific questions about objects and events
  • Make predictions based on observations
  • Record observations through drawing and simple words
  • Identify scientists as people who study the world around us

Daily Lesson Plans

Monday: Meet a Scientist (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Ask: "What do you think a scientist does?"
  • Show picture of scientists working (lab coats, magnifying glasses)
  • Announce: "This week, we're all going to be scientists!"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Explain: "Scientists explore and discover things about our world"
  • Show real examples: magnifying glass, measuring tape, notebook
  • Demonstrate how scientists observe: "I see... I notice... I wonder..."
  • Introduce the five senses as scientist tools
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Scientist Role Play: Children put on "scientist hats"
  • Practice scientist observations of classroom objects
  • Use sentence frames: "I see..." "I notice..." "I wonder..."
  • Record first observations in science journals
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Share one thing each scientist observed
  • Chant: "We are scientists! We observe and discover!"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll use our eyes like scientists!"
Materials Needed:
  • Pictures of scientists at work
  • Simple "scientist hats" (construction paper)
  • Magnifying glass, measuring tape, notebook (for demonstration)
  • Science journals (composition books) for each child

Tuesday: Scientists Use Their Eyes (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Review: "What do scientists do?"
  • Point to eyes: "Today we use these scientist tools!"
  • Ask: "What can your eyes tell you about objects?"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Demonstrate careful looking with various objects
  • Model describing what eyes observe: color, size, shape, texture
  • Compare objects: "This one is bigger... This one is red..."
  • Show how scientists look closely and far away
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Observation Station: Mystery objects in bags with holes
  • Children peek through holes and describe what they see
  • Use magnifying glasses to look at leaves, coins, fabric
  • Draw detailed pictures of observations in science journals
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Share most interesting thing seen today
  • Practice: "Scientists use their eyes to..."
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll listen like scientists!"
Materials Needed:
  • Various objects for observation (toys, natural items, household objects)
  • Paper bags with holes cut out
  • Magnifying glasses (at least 4-5)
  • Leaves, coins, fabric scraps for close observation

Wednesday: Scientists Use Their Ears (20 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Close eyes and listen: "What sounds do you hear right now?"
  • Point to ears: "Scientists use these tools too!"
  • Ask: "How can sounds give us information?"
Core Instruction (8 minutes)
  • Demonstrate different sounds with various objects
  • Model describing sounds: loud/quiet, high/low, rough/smooth
  • Show how sounds can tell us about objects (shaking boxes)
  • Discuss how animals and nature make different sounds
Active Practice (7 minutes)
  • Sound Detective Game: Identify mystery sounds
  • Shake containers with different objects inside
  • Make sounds with instruments and describe them
  • Record sound observations using pictures and words
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Share favorite sound discovered today
  • Practice: "Scientists use their ears to..."
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll use our nose and tongue!"
Materials Needed:
  • Various containers with different objects inside (rice, coins, cotton balls)
  • Simple instruments (shakers, bells, rhythm sticks)
  • Audio of nature sounds (optional)
  • Recording sheets with pictures and spaces for words

Thursday: Scientists Smell and Taste Safely (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Point to nose and tongue: "More scientist tools!"
  • Important safety rule: "Only smell and taste things a grown-up says are safe"
  • Ask: "What are your favorite smells? Tastes?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Demonstrate safe smelling with various items
  • Model describing smells: sweet, fresh, strong, mild
  • Taste safe foods and describe: sweet, salty, sour, bitter
  • Discuss how smell and taste help animals find food and stay safe
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Safe Smelling Station: Explore scent containers
  • Taste test with safe foods (crackers, apple, lemon)
  • Match smells to pictures when possible
  • Record favorite and least favorite smells/tastes
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Review safety rule about smelling and tasting
  • Share one new discovery about smell or taste
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll use our hands like scientists!"
Materials Needed:
  • Safe scent containers (vanilla, cinnamon, mint, orange peel)
  • Safe tasting foods (crackers, apple slices, lemon pieces)
  • Cups of water for rinsing between tastes
  • Napkins and hand sanitizer

Friday: Scientists Use Their Hands (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Hold up hands: "Our last scientist tools!"
  • Quick review: "What are all five senses?"
  • Ask: "What can your hands tell you about objects?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Demonstrate feeling different textures and temperatures
  • Model describing touch: smooth/rough, hard/soft, warm/cool
  • Show how hands can tell us about size, weight, and shape
  • Discuss safety: "Always ask before touching unknown objects"
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Touch and Feel Boxes: Identify objects by touch
  • Texture exploration with fabric, sandpaper, cotton, metal
  • Temperature comparison with warm and cool objects
  • Complete "Five Senses Scientist" certificate
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Celebrate: "We are scientists! We used all five senses!"
  • Share most surprising touch discovery
  • Preview next week: "We'll learn about special scientist tools!"
Materials Needed:
  • Touch boxes with various textured objects
  • Fabric scraps, sandpaper, cotton balls, metal objects
  • Warm and cool objects (ice pack, warm water bottle)
  • "Five Senses Scientist" certificates to complete

Week 1 Assessment

What to Observe:

  • Observation Skills: Can the child use their senses to notice details about objects?
  • Scientific Language: Does the child use describing words (color, size, texture)?
  • Question Asking: Does the child ask "I wonder..." questions about what they observe?
  • Recording: Can the child draw or write simple observations?
  • Scientific Thinking: Does the child show curiosity and careful attention?

Success Indicators:

  • Child uses all five senses during observations
  • Child describes objects using specific details
  • Child asks questions about what they observe
  • Child records observations through pictures and words
  • Child demonstrates enthusiasm for scientific exploration

Home Connection Activities

For Families This Week:

  • Nature Walk: Take a "scientist walk" and observe with all five senses
  • Kitchen Science: Smell spices, feel different textures of foods safely
  • Sound Hunt: Listen for different sounds around your home and neighborhood
  • Texture Collection: Gather objects with different textures (smooth, rough, soft, hard)
  • Science Journal: Help your child draw or describe one observation each day

Questions to Ask Your Child:

  • "What do you notice about this?"
  • "How does this feel/sound/smell?"
  • "What questions do you have about this?"
  • "What would a scientist do?"
