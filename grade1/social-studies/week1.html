Week 1: All About Me

Discovering What Makes Each Person Special and Unique

‚Üê Back to Grade 1 Social Studies

Week Learning Goals

  • Identify personal characteristics that make each individual unique
  • Describe family relationships and their importance
  • Express personal preferences and interests confidently
  • Recognize individual strengths and achievements
  • Show respect for differences among classmates and community members

Daily Lesson Plans

Monday: My Name and Family (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Welcome circle: "Today we learn about what makes YOU special!"
  • Ask: "What is the most important word you know?" (your name)
  • Chant: "Every person is special, every person is unique!"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Discuss: "Your name belongs only to you - it's special!"
  • Show how names can have special meanings or honor family members
  • Talk about families: "Families care for each other and help us grow"
  • Explain different types of families (big, small, different members)
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Name Art: Decorate first letter of name with patterns and colors
  • Practice writing full name clearly
  • Share family photos or draw family members
  • Interview partner: "Tell me about your family"
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Circle share: "My name is __ and my family helps me by __"
  • Affirm: "Each name and each family is special"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll discover what we're each good at!"
Materials Needed:
  • Large paper for name art
  • Crayons, markers, colored pencils
  • Family photos (if available) or drawing paper
  • Name cards for tracing practice

Tuesday: My Talents and Strengths (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Ask: "What is something you do really well?"
  • Explain: "Everyone has special talents and strengths"
  • Show examples: running, drawing, helping others, reading
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Discuss different types of talents: physical, creative, social, academic
  • Emphasize: "Some people are good at different things - that's wonderful!"
  • Model sharing: "I am good at... because I practice and try hard"
  • Connect talents to helping others and contributing to community
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Talent Show and Tell: Each child demonstrates or describes a talent
  • Create "I Am Good At" booklets with pictures and words
  • Practice giving specific compliments to classmates
  • Draw themselves doing their favorite skill
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Applaud everyone's shared talents
  • Affirm: "Every person has valuable abilities"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll talk about our favorite things!"
Materials Needed:
  • Small booklets or folded paper for "I Am Good At" books
  • Drawing materials
  • Props for demonstrations (optional)
  • Stickers or stamps for positive recognition

Wednesday: My Favorites and Interests (20 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Quick share: "What's your favorite color? Why?"
  • Observe: "We all have different favorites - that's interesting!"
  • Ask: "Why do you think people like different things?"
Core Instruction (8 minutes)
  • Explore different categories: foods, activities, books, games, seasons
  • Discuss: "Our interests help us learn and grow in different ways"
  • Model making choices: "I like this because..."
  • Emphasize: "It's good to try new things and discover new interests"
Active Practice (7 minutes)
  • Favorites Gallery Walk: Stations for different categories
  • Create personal "Favorites" chart with pictures and words
  • Interview classmates about their favorites
  • Find someone who shares a favorite and discuss why they like it
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Share one surprising thing learned about a classmate
  • Celebrate: "So many different favorites make our class interesting!"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll learn about our goals and dreams!"
Materials Needed:
  • Chart paper divided into categories (food, activities, books, etc.)
  • Magazines for cutting pictures (optional)
  • Glue sticks and scissors
  • Interview recording sheets

Thursday: My Goals and Dreams (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Ask: "What do you want to learn this year?"
  • Explain: "Goals are things we want to achieve"
  • Share: "Dreams are big ideas about our future"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Discuss difference between goals (achievable plans) and dreams (big aspirations)
  • Give examples: "Learn to read chapter books" (goal) vs "Become a doctor" (dream)
  • Emphasize: "Hard work and learning help us reach our goals"
  • Connect to individual responsibility: "You are in charge of your learning"
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Goal Setting Activity: Choose one learning goal for this month
  • Create "When I Grow Up" drawings with dream careers
  • Discuss steps needed to reach goals
  • Make goal commitment: "I will work hard to..."
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Share one goal each child chose
  • Encourage: "You can achieve amazing things through effort!"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll celebrate everything special about us!"
Materials Needed:
  • Goal-setting worksheets or templates
  • Drawing paper for "When I Grow Up" pictures
  • Books about different careers (optional)
  • Commitment certificates to sign

Friday: Celebrating Our Uniqueness (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Review the week: "What did we learn about ourselves?"
  • Ask: "How are we all the same? How are we different?"
  • Celebrate: "Our differences make our class community stronger!"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • All About Me Presentations: Each child shares their work from the week
  • Practice giving and receiving compliments respectfully
  • Discuss: "How can we help each other reach our goals?"
  • Emphasize classroom community: "We're all important members"
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Create class "We Are Special" bulletin board display
  • Write positive notes to classmates about their unique qualities
  • Play "Human Scavenger Hunt" - find classmates with shared interests
  • Complete "Certificate of Uniqueness" for each child
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Group affirmation: "We are all special and important!"
  • Reflection: "What's one thing you learned about yourself this week?"
  • Preview next week: "We'll learn about our school community!"
Materials Needed:
  • All work from the week for presentations
  • Bulletin board space and decorative materials
  • Note cards for writing positive messages
  • "Certificate of Uniqueness" templates

Week 1 Assessment

What to Observe:

  • Self-Awareness: Can the child identify their own characteristics, interests, and strengths?
  • Communication: Does the child express their thoughts and preferences clearly?
  • Respect for Others: Does the child listen to and appreciate classmates' differences?
  • Goal Setting: Can the child identify something they want to learn or achieve?
  • Confidence: Does the child show pride in their unique qualities?

Success Indicators:

  • Child shares personal information confidently
  • Child recognizes and celebrates their own strengths
  • Child shows interest in learning about others
  • Child demonstrates respect for classmates' differences
  • Child sets a realistic learning goal and shows commitment

Home Connection Activities

For Families This Week:

  • Family Tree Talk: Share stories about family members and their special qualities
  • Talent Time: Have each family member demonstrate or share a special skill
  • Favorites Discussion: Compare and celebrate different preferences within the family
  • Goal Setting: Help your child identify one thing they want to learn at home
  • Photo Sharing: Look at pictures together and talk about special memories

Questions to Ask Your Child:

  • "What makes you special and unique?"
  • "What did you learn about your classmates this week?"
  • "What goal do you want to work on at home?"
  • "How does our family help you grow and learn?"
