Week 1 Overview

This first week introduces students to the wonderful world of numbers through concrete, hands-on experiences. Children will discover that numbers are everywhere around them and learn to count objects systematically from 1 to 10. The focus is on building one-to-one correspondence and recognizing numerals in context.

Learning Objectives

  • Count objects 1-10 accurately using one-to-one correspondence
  • Recognize numerals 1-10 and match them to quantities
  • Find numbers in the environment and explain their purpose
  • Use counting vocabulary (first, second, last, etc.)
  • Compare small groups (same, more, fewer)

Materials Needed This Week

  • Counting bears, cubes, or similar manipulatives (at least 20 per child)
  • Number cards 1-10
  • Ten frames (empty grids)
  • Various small objects for counting (buttons, beans, shells)
  • Chart paper for class number line
  • Math journals or paper for recording
  • Dot stickers or stamps

Daily Lesson Plans

Monday: Numbers in Our Classroom

Opening Circle (10 minutes)

Question of the Day: "How many eyes do you have?"

  • Count eyes together: 1, 2
  • Count fingers on one hand: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
  • Look around the room: "What can we count?"
Main Activity (20 minutes): Number Hunt

Objective: Find numbers in our classroom environment

  • Walk around classroom looking for written numbers
  • Find: clock, calendar, books, signs, addresses
  • Count objects we see: chairs, windows, pencils
  • Record findings on chart paper

Teacher Notes: Point out that numbers help us organize and measure our world

Hands-On Practice (15 minutes)

Each child gets 10 counting bears:

  • Count bears one by one, touching each bear
  • Make groups of 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
  • Match number cards to bear groups
Closing (5 minutes)

Share one number we found today and where we found it

Tuesday: Counting with Purpose

Opening Circle (10 minutes)

Question of the Day: "How many children are here today?"

  • Count children present by pointing to each one
  • Compare to yesterday's count
  • Practice counting to 10 with claps
Main Activity (20 minutes): Ten Frame Introduction

Objective: Learn to organize counting using ten frames

  • Show large ten frame (2 rows of 5 boxes)
  • Place manipulatives in boxes one at a time
  • Count: "1, 2, 3..." as we fill boxes
  • Practice with numbers 1-5 first, then 6-10

Why ten frames matter: They help children see number patterns and prepare for addition

Independent Practice (15 minutes)

Children work with personal ten frames:

  • Place dot stickers in frames to show numbers 1-5
  • Count aloud as they place each sticker
  • Match number cards to completed frames
Closing (5 minutes)

Show a completed ten frame and have class count together

Wednesday: One-to-One Correspondence

Opening Circle (10 minutes)

Question of the Day: "Do we have enough pencils for everyone?"

  • Give each child one pencil
  • Count children, count pencils
  • Discuss: "Each person gets one pencil"
Main Activity (20 minutes): Matching Games

Objective: Understand that each object gets counted once

  • Activity 1: Bears in cups - one bear per cup
  • Activity 2: Flowers in vases - one flower per vase
  • Activity 3: Dots on paper - touch and count each dot once

Key Concept: Each thing we count gets touched or moved exactly once

Problem Solving (15 minutes)

Scenario: "We have 6 children and 6 cookies. Does everyone get one?"

  • Act it out with real children and cookie cutouts
  • Line up children and cookies
  • Match one-to-one
  • Discuss: same number, different number
Closing (5 minutes)

Practice counting body parts: eyes, ears, hands, feet

Thursday: Number Recognition

Opening Circle (10 minutes)

Question of the Day: "What number comes after 3?"

  • Count together 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
  • Show number cards in order
  • Let children take turns holding cards
Main Activity (20 minutes): Number Card Games

Objective: Connect numeral symbols to quantities

  • Game 1: Number fishing - draw card, show that many fingers
  • Game 2: Number hunt - find the number 3 around the room
  • Game 3: Build the number - use blocks to match the card

Focus: The symbol "3" means the same as three objects

Creative Practice (15 minutes)

Number art project:

  • Each child gets a number card (1-5)
  • Decorate the number with that many stickers
  • If you have "4", put 4 stickers on it
  • Count stickers as you place them
Closing (5 minutes)

Hold up fingers to match number cards shown by teacher

Friday: Celebration and Assessment

Opening Circle (10 minutes)

Question of the Day: "What's your favorite number from 1 to 10?"

  • Each child shares their favorite number
  • Count how many chose each number
  • Make a simple graph on chart paper
Assessment Activity (20 minutes): Number Show and Tell

Individual Assessment: Each child demonstrates understanding

  • Count objects 1-10 accurately
  • Match 3 number cards to correct quantities
  • Find and explain one number in the classroom
  • Show understanding of one-to-one correspondence

Assessment Notes: Observe and record each child's counting accuracy and numeral recognition

Celebration Activity (15 minutes)

Number Museum: Display the week's work

  • Show decorated number cards from Thursday
  • Share favorite discoveries from Monday's number hunt
  • Sing counting songs learned this week
  • Take turns being "number detectives"
Closing (5 minutes)

Preview next week: "Next week we'll learn about which groups have more and which have less!"

Assessment and Evaluation

What to Look For

  • Counting Accuracy: Can the child count objects 1-10 without skipping or double-counting?
  • One-to-One Correspondence: Does the child touch or move each object once when counting?
  • Number Recognition: Can the child identify numerals 1-10 when shown?
  • Quantity Matching: Can the child match numerals to the correct number of objects?
  • Environmental Awareness: Does the child notice numbers in the world around them?

Success Indicators

Meets Expectations:

  • Counts objects 1-10 accurately most of the time
  • Recognizes numerals 1-5 consistently
  • Uses one-to-one correspondence when counting
  • Shows interest in finding numbers in the environment

Exceeds Expectations:

  • Counts accurately beyond 10
  • Recognizes numerals 1-10 quickly and accurately
  • Explains why counting accurately is important
  • Helps other children with counting activities

Extension Activities for Home

Family Number Exploration

  • Number Detective: Find numbers around your house (clocks, addresses, phones)
  • Counting Collections: Count toys, books, or snacks together
  • Number Stories: Make up stories using numbers: "3 little bears had 4 bowls..."
  • Finger Games: Practice showing numbers with fingers
  • Cooking Math: Count ingredients when cooking together

Connecting to Objectivist Principles

Using Your Mind (Reason)

Children learn that counting follows logical rules. Each object is counted once, numbers follow in order, and the final number tells us "how many." They discover these patterns through careful observation and thinking.

Learning Real Things (Reality)

All counting uses real, concrete objects that children can see and touch. Numbers aren't abstract symbols but represent actual quantities in the real world - toys, fingers, snacks, people.

Learning Helps Me (Self-Interest)

Children see immediately how counting helps them: "Do I have enough cookies?" "How many friends are here?" "Which pile has more?" Numbers become useful tools for understanding their world.

I Am Important (Individual Worth)

Each child's counting progress is celebrated individually. Some children may count to 15, others master 1-5. Every achievement matters and builds confidence in their mathematical thinking.
