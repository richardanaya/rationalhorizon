Week 1: Numbers All Around Us

Discovering Numbers 1-10 Through Real Objects

‚Üê Back to Grade 1 Math

Week Learning Goals

  • Count objects 1-10 using one-to-one correspondence
  • Recognize and write numerals 1-10
  • Match quantities to numerals and numerals to quantities
  • Identify numbers in the environment around home and school
  • Use number words correctly in speech (one, two, three, etc.)

Daily Lesson Plans

Monday: Number Hunt (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Counting song: "1, 2, 3, 4, 5, I caught a fish alive!"
  • Show fingers while counting to 5
  • Ask: "What numbers do you see around our classroom?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Walk around classroom pointing to numbers: clock, calendar, books, etc.
  • Count real objects together: chairs, pencils, windows
  • Demonstrate one-to-one correspondence: touch each object while counting
  • Introduce numerals 1-5 on large cards with corresponding dots
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Number Hunt Game: Students find objects in groups of 1-5
  • Use counting bears/blocks to make groups
  • Match numeral cards to object groups
  • Draw 1-5 objects on paper and write the numeral
Closure (2 minutes)
  • "What numbers did we find today?"
  • Count together 1-5 using fingers
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll discover numbers 6-10!"
Materials Needed:
  • Large numeral cards 1-5
  • Counting bears or blocks (at least 15 per child)
  • Paper and crayons
  • Dot stickers or stamps

Tuesday: Bigger Numbers 6-10 (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Review counting 1-5 with hand motions
  • Ask: "What comes after 5?"
  • Show both hands: "How many fingers do I have?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Count from 1-10 using both hands
  • Introduce numerals 6-10 with ten-frame cards
  • Count larger groups: books, markers, students
  • Show how 6 is "5 and 1 more", 7 is "5 and 2 more", etc.
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Make groups of 6-10 with manipulatives
  • Ten Frame Activity: Fill frames with objects
  • Number formation practice: trace and write 6-10
  • Match numerals to ten-frame pictures
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Count 1-10 together using number line
  • Show me 7 fingers! Show me 9 fingers!
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll practice counting everything!"
Materials Needed:
  • Ten-frame cards and counters
  • Large numeral cards 6-10
  • Number line 1-10
  • Tracing worksheets for numerals 6-10

Wednesday: Counting Practice (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Number song: Count to 10 with clapping
  • Quick review: hold up fingers for each number called
  • Ask: "What's your favorite number 1-10? Why?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Count various classroom objects in different arrangements
  • Demonstrate that count stays same regardless of arrangement
  • Practice careful one-to-one correspondence
  • Count forward and backward from different starting points
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Counting Stations: Rotate through 4 activities
  • Station 1: Count and sort buttons by color
  • Station 2: Roll dice and count dots
  • Station 3: Count objects in pictures
  • Station 4: Make number books (draw quantities 1-10)
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Share one thing each student counted today
  • Practice counting backwards from 10
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll match numbers to groups!"
Materials Needed:
  • Assorted counting objects (buttons, shells, blocks)
  • Dice (large foam dice work well)
  • Picture counting worksheets
  • Blank booklets for number books

Thursday: Number Matching (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Quick count around the circle: each child says next number
  • Show numeral cards quickly - students shout the number
  • Ask: "Can you show me 4 without using your fingers?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Demonstrate matching activities with large materials
  • Show different representations: dots, objects, fingers, numerals
  • Practice "How many?" with various groups
  • Emphasize that the numeral tells us "how many"
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Memory Game: Match numeral cards to dot cards
  • Use number puzzles (numeral connects to picture)
  • Practice with number stamps and ink pads
  • Play "Number Detective" - find all the 7s in the room
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Quick assessment: hold up objects, students write numeral
  • Celebrate discoveries: "I found 3 windows!"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll show everything we learned!"
Materials Needed:
  • Memory game cards (numerals and dots)
  • Number puzzles
  • Number stamps and ink pads
  • Small whiteboards and markers

Friday: Number Celebration (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Special number song with actions
  • Ask each child to share their favorite number discovery this week
  • Review: "What numbers did we learn about?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Number Show and Tell: Students present their number books
  • Class counts together for each presentation
  • Discuss: "Where do we see these numbers at home?"
  • Plan homework: "number hunt" at home with family
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Number Games Choice Time:
  • Free choice from week's activities
  • Partner counting games
  • Complete number books or start new ones
  • Challenge: Can you count to 15? 20?
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Group count to 10 with celebration at the end
  • Reflection: "What was the most fun thing about numbers this week?"
  • Preview next week: "We'll learn which groups have MORE!"
Materials Needed:
  • Completed number books from the week
  • All manipulatives from previous days
  • Stickers or stamps for celebration
  • Take-home "number hunt" activity sheets

Week 1 Assessment

What to Observe:

  • Counting Accuracy: Can the child count objects 1-10 without skipping or double-counting?
  • One-to-One Correspondence: Does the child touch each object once while counting?
  • Numeral Recognition: Can the child identify numerals 1-10 when shown?
  • Numeral Formation: Can the child write numerals 1-10 with proper formation?
  • Quantity Matching: Can the child match numerals to correct quantities?

Success Indicators:

  • Child counts confidently to 10
  • Child recognizes numerals 1-10 quickly
  • Child can create groups matching given numerals
  • Child uses number vocabulary correctly
  • Child shows enthusiasm for number activities

Home Connection Activities

For Families This Week:

  • Number Hunt: Look for numbers around your home - clocks, house numbers, TV channels
  • Counting Snacks: Count crackers, grapes, or other snacks before eating
  • Bedtime Counting: Count toys as you put them away
  • Number Stories: Read counting books like "Ten Black Dots" or "Chicka Chicka 1, 2, 3"
  • Finger Games: Practice showing different numbers with fingers

Questions to Ask Your Child:

  • "How many ___ do you see?"
  • "Can you find the number 7?"
  • "What number comes after 5?"
  • "Show me 3 with your fingers."
