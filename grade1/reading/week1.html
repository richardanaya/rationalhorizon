Week 1: Letter Recognition

Mastering Letters A-E: The Building Blocks of Reading

‚Üê Back to Grade 1 Reading

Week Learning Goals

  • Identify uppercase and lowercase letters A, B, C, D, E
  • Form letters correctly using proper strokes and direction
  • Recognize letters in sequence and alphabetical order
  • Associate letters with beginning sounds in simple words
  • Distinguish between similar letters (like b and d)

Daily Lesson Plans

Monday: Letters A and B (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Alphabet song to set the mood
  • Show large letter cards A and B
  • Ask: "What letter do you think we'll learn first?"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Letter A: Show uppercase and lowercase, trace in air
  • Words that start with A: apple, ant, alligator (show pictures)
  • Letter B: Show forms, practice "ball bouncing" motion
  • Words that start with B: ball, bear, banana (show pictures)
  • Letter formation: A starts at the top, B has two bumps
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Finger-trace letters A and B in sand/salt trays
  • Practice writing A and B on large paper with thick crayons
  • Letter hunt: find A and B in alphabet books
  • Body letters: make A and B shapes with arms
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Quick review: "Show me A! Show me B!"
  • Name something that starts with A or B
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll meet letters C and D!"
Materials Needed:
  • Large letter cards A, B (upper and lowercase)
  • Picture cards: apple, ant, alligator, ball, bear, banana
  • Sand/salt trays for finger tracing
  • Large paper and thick crayons
  • Alphabet books

Tuesday: Letters C and D (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Quick review: hold up A and B cards
  • Alphabet song to letter D
  • Ask: "What comes after B in the alphabet?"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Letter C: "C is like a circle with a bite taken out"
  • Words with C: cat, car, cup (show pictures and emphasize /k/ sound)
  • Letter D: "D has a straight line and a curve"
  • Words with D: dog, door, duck (show pictures)
  • Compare b and d: "They face different directions"
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Clay letter forming: roll and shape C and D
  • Letter sorting: put C and D cards in correct piles
  • Directional practice: b faces this way, d faces that way
  • Writing practice on dotted line paper
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Memory game: show letter, students name it
  • Share a word that starts with C or D
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll learn our last letter this week!"
Materials Needed:
  • Letter cards C, D (upper and lowercase)
  • Picture cards: cat, car, cup, dog, door, duck
  • Play clay or playdough
  • Dotted line writing paper
  • Letter sorting mats

Wednesday: Letter E and Review (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Flash card review: A, B, C, D
  • Ask: "What letter comes next?"
  • Anticipation: "This letter is in the word 'elephant'!"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Letter E: "E has three lines across and one line down"
  • Words with E: elephant, egg, envelope (show pictures)
  • Review all five letters: A, B, C, D, E
  • Practice alphabetical order with letter cards
  • Focus on proper letter formation for each
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Letter Race: Students find letters A-E hidden around room
  • Complete letter formation practice sheets
  • Match uppercase to lowercase versions
  • Beginning sound sorting: put pictures under correct letters
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Sing A-B-C-D-E song
  • Quick check: "Show me the letter that starts 'elephant'"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll put our letters together!"
Materials Needed:
  • Letter card E (upper and lowercase)
  • Picture cards: elephant, egg, envelope
  • All previous letter cards A-D
  • Letter formation practice sheets
  • Picture sorting mats

Thursday: Letter Practice and Games (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Challenge: "Can you say the alphabet to E without singing?"
  • Show letters randomly - students name them quickly
  • Ask: "Which letter is your favorite so far?"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Review proper letter formation with step-by-step modeling
  • Focus on common mistakes: starting letters at bottom, wrong direction
  • Practice distinguishing b from d with memory tricks
  • Emphasize starting letters at the top
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Letter Bingo: Call out letters, students cover them
  • Writing race: who can write A-E neatly first?
  • Letter memory game: flip cards to match upper and lowercase
  • Picture-letter matching with multiple choices
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Group assessment: hold up letters, class responds
  • Celebrate improvements: "Look how well you're writing!"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll show off everything we learned!"
Materials Needed:
  • Letter bingo cards and markers
  • Timers for writing races
  • Memory game cards (upper/lowercase matches)
  • Multiple choice picture cards
  • Pencils and good writing paper

Friday: Letter Celebration and Assessment (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Special alphabet song with actions for A-E
  • Ask students to share their favorite letter from this week
  • Anticipation: "Let's show what excellent letter learners we are!"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Student Presentations: Each child shows their best letter
  • Demonstrate letter formation for the class
  • Share a word that starts with their chosen letter
  • Class gives encouragement and positive feedback
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Choice Centers: Students pick favorite activities
  • Letter art: decorate letters with stickers and colors
  • Free writing practice
  • Letter games from the week
  • Create alphabet books starting with A-E
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Group recitation: A, B, C, D, E with confidence
  • Reflection: "What was most fun about learning letters?"
  • Preview next week: "We'll learn letter sounds F, G, H, I, J!"
Materials Needed:
  • Stickers and decorating materials
  • All manipulatives and games from the week
  • Blank alphabet books
  • Assessment checklist for observation
  • Celebration stickers or stamps

Week 1 Assessment

Letter Recognition Checklist:

  • Uppercase Recognition: Can identify A, B, C, D, E when shown randomly
  • Lowercase Recognition: Can identify a, b, c, d, e when shown randomly
  • Letter Formation: Writes letters with correct starting point and direction
  • Sequence Knowledge: Can recite A, B, C, D, E in order
  • Beginning Sounds: Can name words that start with each letter

Success Indicators:

  • Child recognizes 4/5 letters consistently
  • Child can write 3/5 letters with proper formation
  • Child distinguishes between b and d
  • Child shows enthusiasm for letter activities
  • Child can match some uppercase to lowercase letters

Support Needed If:

  • Child confuses more than 2 letters consistently
  • Child starts letters from the bottom
  • Child cannot distinguish b from d
  • Child shows frustration with writing activities

Home Connection Activities

For Families This Week:

  • Letter Hunt: Find letters A-E in books, signs, and magazines
  • Kitchen Letters: Practice writing letters in spilled flour or finger paint
  • Bedtime ABCs: Review A, B, C, D, E before bed
  • Letter Books: Read alphabet books focusing on A-E
  • Name Games: Find family names that start with A, B, C, D, or E

Questions to Ask Your Child:

  • "Can you show me how to write the letter B?"
  • "What letter does your name start with?"
  • "Can you find the letter A on this page?"
  • "What word starts with the same letter as 'cat'?"

Recommended Books:

  • "Chicka Chicka Boom Boom" by Bill Martin Jr.
  • "The Z Was Zapped" by Chris Van Allsburg
  • "Dr. Seuss's ABC" by Dr. Seuss
  • "Alphabet City" by Stephen T. Johnson
