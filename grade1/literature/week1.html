Week 1: The Three Little Pigs

Hard Work and Preparation Pay Off

‚Üê Back to Grade 1 Literature

Week Learning Goals

  • Understand story structure with beginning, middle, and end
  • Identify characters and setting in the story
  • Recognize the theme that hard work and preparation lead to success
  • Make connections between story events and real life
  • Retell the story in correct sequence using key details

Daily Lesson Plans

Monday: Meet the Characters (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Show cover of "The Three Little Pigs" book
  • Ask: "What do you see? What do you think this story will be about?"
  • Introduce: "This week we'll learn about building strong foundations"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Read first part of story, focusing on the three pigs as characters
  • Discuss: "Characters are the people or animals in a story"
  • Identify each pig and their different approaches to building
  • Introduce setting: "Where and when the story takes place"
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Character Cards: Draw and label the three pigs
  • Describe each pig's personality with simple words
  • Act out being each pig (quick movements, different voices)
  • Predict: "What do you think will happen next?"
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Review: "Who are the characters in our story?"
  • Ask: "Which pig do you think is wisest? Why?"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll see what materials they use to build!"
Materials Needed:
  • "The Three Little Pigs" book (large format preferred)
  • Paper for character cards
  • Crayons or markers
  • Simple props for acting (optional)

Tuesday: Building Materials Matter (20 minutes)

Opening (2 minutes)
  • Quick review: "What did each pig decide to do?"
  • Show real examples: straw, sticks, toy bricks
  • Ask: "Which material do you think is strongest?"
Core Instruction (10 minutes)
  • Continue reading story, focusing on each house being built
  • Discuss different materials: straw (quick, easy), sticks (medium effort), bricks (hard work)
  • Connect to real life: "What are real houses made of?"
  • Emphasize: "The pig who worked hardest built the strongest house"
Active Practice (6 minutes)
  • Material Testing: Feel and compare actual straw, sticks, and blocks
  • Build simple structures with each material
  • Test strength: "Which one stands up best?"
  • Draw the three houses with different materials
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Compare results: "Which material was strongest?"
  • Connect: "How is this like working hard in school?"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow the wolf arrives! What will happen?"
Materials Needed:
  • Real or craft straw/hay
  • Small sticks or craft sticks
  • Building blocks or toy bricks
  • Drawing paper for house illustrations

Wednesday: The Wolf Arrives (20 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Dramatic entrance: "Here comes the big bad wolf!"
  • Review: "Which houses have been built?"
  • Ask: "What do you think the wolf wants?"
Core Instruction (8 minutes)
  • Read the exciting part where the wolf tries to blow down each house
  • Emphasize cause and effect: weak materials = house falls down
  • Discuss the wolf as a problem the pigs must solve
  • Highlight: "Only the brick house stands strong"
Active Practice (7 minutes)
  • Wolf and Houses Drama: Act out the story with enthusiasm
  • Practice huffing and puffing (great for phonics!)
  • Test our built structures by "blowing" on them
  • Sequence cards: Put story events in order
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Discuss: "Why couldn't the wolf blow down the brick house?"
  • Connect: "Hard work protected the third pig"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll see how the story ends!"
Materials Needed:
  • Simple costumes or props (wolf ears, pig noses)
  • Structures built on Tuesday
  • Sequence cards with story events
  • Fan or hair dryer for "wind" effect (optional)

Thursday: Problem Solved! (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Review: "What happened to the first two houses?"
  • Ask: "How do you think the story will end?"
  • Predict: "Will the wolf get into the brick house?"
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Finish reading the story - wolf's defeat and pigs' safety
  • Discuss the problem and solution: "How did the third pig solve the problem?"
  • Identify the story's lesson: "Hard work and planning ahead pay off"
  • Connect to real life: "When do we need to work hard to be safe or successful?"
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Story Map Creation: Beginning, middle, end with pictures
  • Discussion: "What did each pig learn?"
  • Personal connection: "When did hard work help you?"
  • Alternative endings: "What if the first pig had used bricks?"
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Moral discussion: "What does this story teach us?"
  • Share: "How can you work hard like the third pig?"
  • Preview: "Tomorrow we'll retell the whole story!"
Materials Needed:
  • Story map templates (beginning, middle, end sections)
  • Drawing and writing materials
  • Chart paper for class discussion
  • Stickers or stamps for completing story maps

Friday: Retelling and Celebrating (25 minutes)

Opening (3 minutes)
  • Challenge: "Who can tell me what happened first in our story?"
  • Introduce retelling: "Telling a story in your own words"
  • Review story structure: beginning, middle, end
Core Instruction (12 minutes)
  • Guided Class Retelling: Tell story together using props and pictures
  • Practice using sequence words: "First," "Then," "Next," "Finally"
  • Emphasize key details and story lesson
  • Individual retelling practice with partners
Active Practice (8 minutes)
  • Create "Three Little Pigs" stick puppets
  • Use puppets to retell story to a partner
  • Make class book: each child illustrates one part
  • Celebrate hard workers: "Brick House Award" certificates
Closure (2 minutes)
  • Group chant: "Work hard, build strong, success will come along!"
  • Reflection: "How will you be like the wise third pig this week?"
  • Preview next week: "We'll meet another character who works hard!"
Materials Needed:
  • Popsicle sticks and character cutouts for puppets
  • All props and pictures from the week
  • Blank class book or large paper for illustrations
  • "Brick House Award" certificates

Week 1 Assessment

What to Observe:

  • Story Comprehension: Can the child identify main characters, setting, and key events?
  • Sequencing: Does the child retell events in correct order?
  • Theme Understanding: Can the child explain the lesson about hard work?
  • Personal Connections: Does the child relate story events to their own life?
  • Vocabulary: Does the child use story vocabulary correctly?

Success Indicators:

  • Child identifies all three pigs and the wolf as characters
  • Child explains why the brick house was strongest
  • Child retells story with beginning, middle, and end
  • Child connects hard work in story to real-life situations
  • Child demonstrates enthusiasm for the story and its message

Home Connection Activities

For Families This Week:

  • Story Retelling: Have your child retell "The Three Little Pigs" to family members
  • Building Challenge: Build structures with different materials around the house
  • Hard Work Discussion: Share times when hard work helped your family
  • Reading Together: Find other versions of "The Three Little Pigs" to compare
  • Real-Life Connections: Point out strong buildings and discuss what makes them sturdy

Questions to Ask Your Child:

  • "What happened first/next/last in the story?"
  • "Why was the third pig's house the strongest?"
  • "What does this story teach us about working hard?"
  • "When do you work hard like the third pig?"
